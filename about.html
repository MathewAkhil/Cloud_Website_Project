I am a student at Texas A&M class of 2024 pursuing a B.S. in Computer Science and a minor in Statistics. I have interests in machine learning and software development. I am part of the Engineering Honors Program at the university and I am a part of Thrive SAIV.


I was born in Kattappana, India and lived there for a couple of years before moving with my parents to Oman. I would stay there for the next four years before moving to the United States. When I first came to America, I lived in Durham, North Carolina. I would continue this pattern of moving around after a couple of years of staying in the same place, going wherever my parents job led us to. All this moving around made me a really social person as I had to acquire the ability to make friends quickly. In 2011, I moved to Fort Worth, Texas and stayed there for the remainder of elementary school before moving to Lewisville, Texas, which is where I would remain until college.


I was not introduced to computer programming until my freshman year of high school at Hebron High School. I initially was hesitant to take that course but my dad was able to convince me to try it out. Little did I know, that small decision would set me on the path to my current career. Taking that class ignited a flame within me that I had never felt before. I can honestly say that I fell in love with computer programming because of that class. We learned everything in Java for this class and while it was tough to learn a new language, it was a challenge I enjoyed completing. Eventually I would go on to take AP Computer Science, which further solidified my interest in the field of computer science.


When it was time to look for colleges, I searched everywhere to find a good engineering school with an outstanding computer science department. In my search, I happened to stumble upon Texas A&M. They had a very prestigious engineering school with an amazing computer science department as well as outstanding faculty. I decided to take a campus tour and after seeing everything with my own eyes, I knew that Texas A&M was the school for me.
